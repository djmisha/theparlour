# The Parlour — Writing Style Guide

> For use by LLMs, copywriters, and content creators working on The Parlour website and brand materials.

---

## Voice & Identity

The Parlour is the world's most exclusive society of magic — an ancient, secretive order of master practitioners that has existed since the dawn of the conjuring arts. The voice is that of an institution that does not need to prove itself. It speaks with quiet authority, deep reverence for the craft, and the confidence of something that has endured for centuries.

We do not sell. We do not recruit. We do not explain ourselves to the uninitiated. The writing should feel like a door being opened just enough to glimpse what lies within — never flung wide.

---

## Tone

- **Authoritative but not arrogant.** The Parlour knows what it is. It doesn't boast — it states.
- **Mystical but grounded.** The language evokes ancient tradition and sacred ritual, but never tips into fantasy or camp. This is a real society with real practitioners.
- **Exclusive but inviting.** The exclusivity is framed as a promise of quality, not a barrier. The reader should feel drawn in, not shut out.
- **Reverent toward the craft.** Magic is always treated as a serious, sacred art form — never as entertainment, tricks, or novelty.
- **Poetic but precise.** Sentences should have rhythm and weight. Avoid fluff. Every word earns its place.

---

## Key Rhetorical Devices

### The "is not X — it is Y" Construction
The signature rhetorical move of The Parlour. Used to reframe expectations and elevate meaning:
- "The Parlour is not a club. It is a society."
- "Membership is not a transaction — it is a lifelong covenant."
- "A nomination is not a campaign — it is a recognition."
- "This is not secrecy for its own sake — it is the foundation upon which trust is built."

Use this construction once or twice per page. It should feel like a revelation, not a formula.

### Italicized Codas
Each major content section should close with an italicized `<em>` paragraph — a short, poetic summary that seals the section with gravitas. These are fragments or declarations, not explanations:
- *"Those who belong, know. Those who seek to join must prove they are worthy of the secrets within."*
- *"The Parlour does not create masters. It reveals them."*
- *"The doors open only for those who have earned the right to enter."*

### Conditional Invitations
CTAs use conditional, invitational language rather than imperative commands. The reader is invited to consider whether they belong, not told to sign up:
- "If the craft calls to you — if you have spent years in pursuit of the impossible and seek a place among those who understand — then perhaps it is time to make yourself known."
- "If you believe that magic is not merely entertainment but an ancient and sacred art — then perhaps The Parlour has been waiting for you."

### Sentence Rhythm
Alternate between long, flowing sentences (20-40 words) and short, punchy declarations (5-12 words). The short sentences come at the end of paragraphs for emphasis:
- "Many petition. Few are chosen."
- "The Council alone decides."
- "The greatest secret of The Parlour is not any single trick or method. It is the society itself."

## Vocabulary

### Preferred Terms
| Use | Instead of |
|---|---|
| the art / the craft | magic tricks, illusions |
| practitioner | magician (in most contexts) |
| conjury / conjurer | conjuring / conjuror |
| gathering | meeting, event |
| petition | application, sign-up |
| admitted / granted admission | accepted, approved |
| The Council | the board, leadership team |
| The Vault | the  initiation | onboarding, registration |
| brotherhood / fellowship | community, group |
| bestowed | given, awarded |
| summoned | contacted, invited (for formal contexts) |

### Power Words (use frequently)
- **Secrecy lexicon:** sacred, shrouded, concealed, forbidden, guarded, veiled, shadow
- **Mastery lexicon:** mastery, devoted, unwavering, dedication, excellence, discipline
- **Temporal depth:** ancient, centuries, generations, lineage, unbroken chain, since the dawn of
- **Spatial enclosure:** within these walls, behind closed doors, sanctuary, inner sanctum
- **Merit language:** worthy, earned, ilege, distinction, proven, recognized
- **Upward movement:** elevate, ascend, rise, pinnacle, highest form
- **Binding language:** covenant, oath, bond, brotherhood, calling

### Words & Phrases to Avoid
- "Sign up," "join now," "subscribe," "register"
- "Fun," "cool," "awesome," "amazing"
- "Tricks," "stunts," "gimmicks"
- "Customers," "users," "clients"
- "Affordable," "discount," "deal," "free"
- "Check out," "don't miss," "hurry"
- Any urgency-based marketing language
- Exclamation points (except in the 404 page)
- Emojis in body copy

## Metaphor Clusters

Draw from these five metaphor families consistently:

1. **Fire & Light** — "the flame," "keepers of the flame," "burns with an intensity," "the ember passed from one generation to the next"
2. **Veils & Shadows** — "shrouded," "from the shadows," "the veil be lifted," "behind closed doors," "concealed"
3. **Paths & Journeys** — "the path begins," "your journey leads here," "a single step," "ever-unfolding journey," "the threshold"
4. **Sacred & Ritual** — "sacred," "covenant," "oath," "initiation rite," "communion," "calling," "rite of succession"
5. **Royalty & Aristocracy** — "the aristocrat's art," "courts of medieval kings," "bestowed," "distinction"

## Page Structure Conventions

### Hero Sections
- `<h1>`: 2-4 word title, large and dramatic
- `<p class="lead">`: A single poetic fragment (not a full sentence). Examples: "A privilege, not a right" / "The guardians of our tradition"
- A thin gold divider line separates the h1 from the subtitle
- Subtle fade-up entrance animation on load

### Section Headings
- `<h2>`: Declarative, thematic ("Who We Are," "Behind Closed Doors," "The Rite of Succession")
- `<h3>`: More evocative, inside content cards ("Stewards of the Art," "Identities Unknown. Authority Absolute.")

### Content Flow
Each page follows this general arc:
1. Hero (identity statement)
2. Primary content section (who/what/why)
3. Supporting detail sections (philosophy, process, features)
4. Call to action (invitational, never pushy)
5. Section divider

### CTA Sections
- Always the final content section before the page-ending divider
- Heading is evocative, never generic: "Do You Belong?", "The Invitation Awaits", "The Path Begins Here"
- Body copy uses conditional/invitational language
- Button labels: "Request an Invitation" or "Submit Your Petition" (keep consistent)
- Centered on mobile, max-width constrained

## The Parlour's Origin Myth

When referencing the society's history, maintain these narrative threads:
- The Parlour predates all modern magical institutions — it stretches back centuries, not decades
- Origins trace to an unbroken chain of practitioners from ancient civilizations through medieval courts to the present
- The society moved westward when European magical societies fractured in the 18th century
- No member has ever publicly acknowledged involvement
- No roster has been published, no gathering photographed
- The society exists "in the space between what is known and what is believed"
- Master magicians throughout history are whispered to have been part of this lineage, but none have confirmed it

## Content Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Don't say "we are exclusive." Describe the process, the secrecy, the standards — and let the reader feel the exclusivity.
2. **Elevate, don't explain.** The reader should feel they are being granted a glimpse into something extraordinary, not reading an FAQ.
3. **Respect the mystery.** Never over-explain. Leave space for wonder. The best copy on this site is the copy that makes the reader want to know more.
4. **Consistency of register.** Every page — even functional ones like resources or patronage — should maintain the same voice. A PayPal button description should still sound like The Parlour.
5. **The Parlour is above, not among.** Never position The Parlour as one of many magical societies. It is the original. Others are "the latest organizations that have formed within our time." The Parlour goes beyond generations.

## Formatting Notes

- Use em dashes (—) for parenthetical asides and dramatic pauses, not hyphens or en dashes
- Use `<em>` for italicized coda paragraphs, not for general emphasis
- Spell out numbers in prose ("centuries" not "100s of years")e Oxford comma
- "The Parlour" is always capitalized with the article
- "The Council," "The Vault," "The Collection" — all proper nouns with "The"
- "conjury" not "conjuring" (archaic spelling, deliberate)
- No period after hero subtitle fragments
